Discourse-Wizard



Discourse-Wizard is to demonstrate the context in language which comes from their sequential patterns. For example, a sentence is formed by a sequence of words, a conversation is formed by a sequence of utterances, and so on. Dialogue act represents a performative action of the utterance. There have been many approaches to model such a deep contextual-concept. The neural approaches have been deployed to achieve state-of-the-art results.

Smae utterance can be used in different context


As you can see in the small conversation example, Utt2 and Utt4 are very same (in this case same “Yeah”), however, the dialogue acts are very different. As they come from their context utterances Utt1 and Utt3 respectively. If it appears after a ‘Yes-No Question’ dialogue act it is more likely to have ‘Yes-Answer’ rather than ‘Backchannel’ or any other dialogue act. The presented example is from the Switchboard Dialogue Act (SwDA) Corpus, which is annotated with 42 such dialogue acts.

Features:

  • Discourse/conversational analysis
  • Dialogue act recognition
  • Context-based dialogue act recognition
